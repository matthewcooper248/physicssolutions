two lenses

problem

There are two focusing lenses on axis as depicted in the figure below. An object is \(30.0~\mathrm{cm}\) behind the first lens which has a focusing length of \(20.0~\mathrm{cm}\). The distance between the two lenses is \(40.0~\mathrm{cm}\). Determine the location of the image after the two lenses and the total magnification after the two lenses.



solution

The lens equation is given by the following.

\begin{equation}\frac{1}{f}=\frac{1}{d_0}+\frac{1}{d_i}\tag{1}\end{equation}

For the first lens, we will add a 1 subscript to each element.

\begin{equation}\frac{1}{f_1}=\frac{1}{d_{01}}+\frac{1}{d_{i1}}\tag{2}\end{equation}

Now let's plug in the values from the explanation of the problem.

\begin{equation}\frac{1}{20.0~\mathrm{cm}}=\frac{1}{\mathrm{30.0~\mathrm{cm}}}+\frac{1}{d_{i1}}\tag{3}\end{equation} \begin{equation}\frac{1}{d_{i1}}=\frac{1}{20.0~mc}-\frac{1}{30.0~cm}\tag{4}\end{equation} \begin{equation}d_{i1}=\left[\frac{1}{20.0~\mathrm{cm}}-\frac{1}{30.0~\mathrm{cm}}\right]^{-1}\tag{5}\end{equation} \begin{equation}d_{i1}=60.0~\mathrm{cm}\tag{6}\end{equation}

The image, \(d_{01}\), is \(60~\mathrm{cm}\) to the right of the first lens. That means that the image from the first lens is \(20~\mathrm{cm}\) to the right of the second lens since the sencod lens is the \(40~\mathrm{cm}\) to the right of the first lens. Now let's apply the lens equation to the second lens. The distance of the new object from the second lens is now \(-20~\mathrm{cm}\). We get the following.

\begin{equation}\frac{1}{f_2}=\frac{1}{d_{02}}+\frac{1}{d_{i2}}\tag{7}\end{equation} \begin{equation}\frac{1}{40.0~\mathrm{cm}}=\frac{1}{-20.0~\mathrm{cm}}+\frac{1}{d_{i2}}\tag{8}\end{equation} \begin{equation}\frac{1}{d_{i2}}=\frac{1}{40.0~\mathrm{cm}}+\frac{1}{20.0~\mathrm{cm}}\tag{9}\end{equation} \begin{equation}d_{i2}=\left[\frac{1}{40.0~cm}+\frac{1}{20.0~\mathrm{cm}}\right]^{-1}\tag{10}\end{equation} \begin{equation}d_{i2}=13.3~\mathrm{cm}\tag{11}\end{equation}

So the image of the object after the second lens is \(13.3~\mathrm{cm}\) to the right of the second lens. We have determined the location of the image after the two lenses. Now let's determine the magnification of the image after the two lenses. The general equation for magnification, \(M\), is the following.

\begin{equation}M=-\frac{d_i}{d_0}\tag{12}\end{equation}

\(d_i\) is the distance of the image from the lens and \(d_0\) is the distance of the object from the lens. The magnification from the first lens is the following.

\begin{equation}M_1=-\frac{d_{i1}}{d_{01}}\tag{13}\end{equation}

The magnification due to the second lens is the following.

\begin{equation}M_2=-\frac{d_{i2}}{d_{02}}\tag{14}\end{equation}

The total magnification after both lenses is given by the following.

\begin{equation}M=M_1M_2\tag{15}\end{equation}

This becomes the following.

\begin{equation}M=\frac{d_{i1}}{d_{01}}\frac{d_{i2}}{d_{02}}\tag{16}\end{equation}

The magnification in our situation is the following.

\begin{equation}M=\frac{60.0~\mathrm{cm}}{30.0~\mathrm{cm}}\frac{13.3~\mathrm{cm}}{-20.0~\mathrm{cm}}\tag{17}\end{equation} \begin{equation}M=-1.33\tag{18}\end{equation}
